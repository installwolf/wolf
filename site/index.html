Straight answers

The questions you're actually asking.

Can't I just bypass it if I really want to?

Eventually, if you're determined enough, yes, and we say so plainly above. The point isn't a perfect cage. It's to make bypass so slow and so visible that the craving passes before you can pull it off. That gap is where recovery happens.

Does it really work with my VPN on?

Yes. The on-device filter reads each connection's true destination before it ever reaches the tunnel, so ExpressVPN or any other VPN, DNS-over-HTTPS, and iCloud Private Relay can't route around it. We built this layer specifically because it's how people slip past weaker blockers.

Is my browsing tracked or sent anywhere?

No. Everything runs on your Mac. Wolf checks connections against your own blocklist locally and phones nothing home. Your accountability partner sees only the events you'd expect (a removal request, a break-glass), never your history.

What if I lose the partner passphrase?

You wait out the 48-hour cooldown, which removes any site without a passphrase, or you use the break-glass command. Break-glass always works so you can never be locked out of your own machine. It just wipes the setup and leaves a record, so it's a real exit, not a loophole.

Do I need to be technical to use it?

You install it once and manage sites from a small menu-bar app or a one-line command. Setting the partner passphrase is the one step best done by the other person. If you can install a Mac app, you can run Wolf.

Can I get a refund?

If Wolf doesn't hold up its end within 30 days, email us and we'll refund you. We'd rather lose the sale than pretend it fit when it didn't.
